Responsive Redesign
lalalalala

Overview

The goal of this assignment was to take a website and understand its issues and underlying problems. From there, wireframes, visual designs, and prototypes were created to aid in creating a redesign of the website. This redesign addresses the observed issues of the site.

Original Website

Research

A website for New World Montessori School was chosen for its dated interface and inaccessible features.

I've listed the observations below:

- Font not legible without zooming in.
- Clashing colors make the site lose a sense of cohesion.
- Text covers most of the screen, making the site overwhelming and cluttered.
- Menu feels hidden in header.
- Lack of search bar limits user's ability to navigate site.
- Images have dated effects, and unclean cutouts.
- Images don't have alt text.
- Low contrast makes it difficult to differentiate certain text and sections. Especially on the "CULTURALLY DIVERSIFIED" heading.
- Unordered lists.
High-fidelity redesign

Design Interations

When thinking about the new design for the interface, I needed to understand the user experience and how the current state of the interface interfered with its purpose. I turned to interfaces of other schools and particularly preschools to understand what worked well on other sites, and what didn't.

Conclusion

One of the key components of this project was incorporating responsiveness in the redesign. This is something that I really struggled with. Ultimately, I wasn't successful in implementing this. I want to get a better handle on this for future projects.
